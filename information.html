First Aid

1. What Is A First Aid Kit?
A collection of medical supplies used to give immediate care during injuries or emergencies.

2. Where Should I Keep My First Aid Kit?
Keep it in an easy-to-reach place at home, school, or in your bag.

3. How Often Should I Check It?
Check every 3–6 months to replace expired or used items.

4. Can I Use Any Medicine In The Kit Anytime?
No. Only use medicines as needed and follow proper dosage instructions.

5. What Should I Do In Serious Emergencies?
Call emergency services immediately before giving first aid.

Medicine

Pain And Fever

Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
Dosage: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hrs (max 4000 mg/day)

Ibuprofen
Dosage: 200–400 mg every 6–8 hrs (max 1200 mg/day OTC)

Allergy And Colds

Cetirizine / Loratadine
Dosage: 10 mg once daily

Phenylephrine (Decongestant)
Dosage: 10 mg every 4 hrs

Antacids

Dosage: 1–2 tablets after meals or as needed

Loperamide
Dosage: 4 mg first, then 2 mg after each loose stool (max 8 mg/day)

Hydration

Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS)
Dosage: Dissolve 1 sachet in water; drink as needed

Topical Antibiotic Ointment

Dosage: Apply 1–3 times daily

Hydrocortisone Cream
Dosage: Apply 1–2 times daily

Some Frequently Asked Questions:

Can I Drink Medicine On An Empty Stomach?
Some medications can irritate the stomach, consume food unless stated otherwise.

What Happens When I Miss A Dose?
Do not double the next dose.

I’m Experiencing Side Effects, What Do I Do?
Consult a healthcare professional.

Wound Care

Cuts And Scrapes

Indoors: Wash your hands, clean the wound, apply bandage.

Outdoors: Rinse with clean water and cover.

Bleeding

Indoors: Apply pressure and elevate.

Outdoors: Use clean material and apply pressure.

Burns

Indoors: Cool with water (no ice).

Outdoors: Cool immediately, no substances.

Sprains And Bruises

Indoors: Rest and apply cold compress.

Outdoors: Avoid movement and seek help.

No Parent?

If Inside House:

Cuts And Scratches

  1. Wash your hands
  2. Rinse wound with clean water
  3. Remove dirt gently
  4. Apply antiseptic
  5. Cover with bandages

Bleeding

  1. Press with clean cloth
  2. Hold for 5–10 minutes
  3. Raise the injured part
  4. Once it stops, clean and cover

Burns

  1. Cool under running water 10–15 minutes
  2. Remove tight items near burn
  3. Cover loosely with clean cloth

Bruises

  1. Apply cold compress (ice wrapped in cloth)
  2. Hold 10–15 minutes

Sprain

  1. Rest the injured part
  2. Apply ice 15–20 minutes
  3. Wrap with bandage (not tight)
  4. Elevate

If Outside House:

Cuts And Scratches

  1. Wash your hands
  2. Rinse wound with clean water
  3. Remove dirt gently
  4. Cover with bandages if available

Bleeding

  1. Press firmly with tissue or clean cloth
  2. Raise the injured part
  3. Ask help if it doesn’t stop

Burns

  1. Find water immediately
  2. Cool the burn
  3. Do not apply random substances

Bruises / Sprain

  1. Rest and avoid walking if painful
  2. Use cold compress
  3. Ask for help
